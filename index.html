0 to 35K: Building an Athlete's Brand from Scratch

The Challenge

Max Mancuso — competitive fitness athlete and content creator — had a compelling story but no audience. When we started, his Instagram sat at 200 followers and his TikTok at 50. The platform had no consistent identity, no content framework, and no growth strategy. The raw material was there. The architecture wasn't.

The Approach
Research & Strategy

I conducted a full audit of comparable creators in the fitness and endurance space, identifying content gaps and audience triggers. I built a content framework centered on Max's authentic story — his transformation, his discipline, his competition journey — and established a platform-native strategy for each channel.

Instagram

I prioritized Reels with high-share triggers: motivational overlays, competition footage, and raw training content. I structured a posting cadence, coached on caption strategy, and tracked post insights obsessively to iterate week over week.

TikTok

I identified that Max's most relatable content was his mindset and daily discipline, not just performance highlights. I coached on caption strategy and platform-native formatting.

Building a Program's Digital Identity — and Winning a National Competition Doing It

The Challenge

Bates Men's Lacrosse had a small, disengaged social following and no consistent content strategy. With 2,800 Instagram followers and low engagement, the program's digital presence didn't reflect the quality of the team or the culture behind it. My role was to change that — and to use social media as a vehicle for something bigger: a national fundraising campaign for cancer research.

The Approach
Content & Design

I built a multi-platform content strategy from scratch across Instagram and X. I produced all graphics in Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop, all video in Premiere Pro and After Effects, and developed a consistent visual identity for the program.

On the Ground

For the Headstrong Foundation's Lax Stache Madness competition, I designed large-format posters with embedded Venmo QR codes and set up donation stations across campus. I built a structured outreach schedule assigning each player specific days to contact family, friends, and alumni with templated messages and shareable graphics.

On Social

I developed a phased three-week posting calendar. Week one: storytelling and education. Week two: progress updates, milestones, and bracket standings for urgency. Week three: countdown energy and last-push calls to action. Every post optimized for shareability — video recaps, story polls, athlete takeovers, and UGC reposts.
